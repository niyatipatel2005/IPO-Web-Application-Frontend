Top Reasons for Life Insurance Claim Rejection

How to Avoid Common Mistakes and Get Your Claim Approved

04 January 2024 • 6 min read

Understanding why claims get rejected can help your loved ones avoid future distress.

Life insurance offers financial security to your loved ones in your absence. But what happens when a claim is rejected? In India, a small percentage of life insurance claims are denied each year, often due to technicalities or misinformation. Knowing these reasons in advance can help policyholders and nominees avoid devastating financial shocks.

Common Reasons for Life Insurance Claim Rejection

  • Non-Disclosure of Medical History: Failing to disclose pre-existing illnesses or habits like smoking, drinking, etc., can result in claim rejection.
  • Delay in Premium Payment: If the policy lapses due to missed premiums and death occurs in that period, the claim will be denied.
  • Incorrect or Incomplete Information: Errors in nominee details, income, or address may cause documentation issues during claim processing.
  • Death within Contestability Period: If the policyholder dies within the first 2 years, the insurer scrutinizes the claim more strictly and may reject it on technical grounds.
  • Suicide Clause: Claims related to suicide within one year of the policy start are not payable as per most policy terms.

How to Avoid Claim Rejection

  • Always disclose correct health and lifestyle information while purchasing a policy.
  • Ensure your premiums are paid on time and never let the policy lapse.
  • Update nominee and contact details regularly.
  • Inform your family about the policy, where documents are kept, and how to claim.
  • Read the policy document carefully, especially clauses like suicide and contestability.

Life insurance is meant to give peace of mind—not confusion. Understanding and avoiding common mistakes ensures your family's future remains protected when they need it most. Be transparent, be updated, and stay insured wisely.

PSUs have shown strong returns with consistent dividend payouts.

Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs) are government-owned companies that operate across critical sectors like oil, energy, defense, banking, and infrastructure. Many of these firms are highly profitable, dividend-paying, and play a key role in India’s economy. PSU stocks have become attractive again due to strong earnings, privatization plans, and stable growth outlook.

What are PSU Stocks?

  • PSU (Public Sector Undertaking) stocks refer to shares of companies where the government holds a majority stake (usually 51% or more).
  • These companies are listed on the stock exchange and are accountable to both the government and shareholders.
  • Sectors include oil & gas, power, mining, finance, defense, and infrastructure.

Why Invest in PSU Stocks?

  • Attractive valuations compared to private peers.
  • Consistent dividend payouts, especially in energy and banking PSUs.
  • Government support ensures stability during downturns.
  • Privatization initiatives increase efficiency and unlock value.

Risks of Investing in PSUs

  • Policy changes and political decisions can impact business direction.
  • Some PSUs face operational inefficiencies and delayed reforms.
  • Stock performance may lag during high-growth private sector rallies.
